Intelligence Protocol Queries

What specific data sources power the Nexus Plan AI Core?

Our AI dynamically leverages a vast, multi-vector data corpus. This includes, but is not limited to: real-time global news feeds (Dow Jones, Reuters, Bloomberg where accessible, plus thousands of niche sources), financial market data APIs (e.g., SEC EDGAR, Alpha Vantage), regulatory databases (FDA, EMA, etc.), academic research portals (arXiv, PubMed, CORE), international patent repositories (USPTO, EPO, WIPO), specialized industry analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester snippets where permissible), social media trend APIs (monitoring aggregate sentiment and keyword velocity), web crawl intelligence (indexed company websites, forums, review aggregators), and government statistical databases (BEA, Eurostat, etc.). The system prioritizes reputable sources and cross-validates findings for enhanced reliability.

How detailed should my 'Intelligence Objective' be for best results?

Think of it as briefing a highly specialized (and incredibly fast) intelligence analyst. Maximum specificity yields maximum value. Include:

  • Clear Subject: The company, product, market, or technology.
  • Scope: Geographic region(s), industry vertical(s), customer segment(s).
  • Key Questions: What specific answers do you need? (e.g., "What is Competitor X's pricing strategy for Product Y in Europe?", "What are the top 3 adoption barriers for Z technology?").
  • Competitors (if applicable): List known competitors or define characteristics.
  • Timeframe: Specify if you need historical analysis, current state, or future projections (e.g., "last 12 months", "next 3 years").
  • Desired Outcome: What decision will this report inform? (e.g., "Inform market entry decision", "Refine product roadmap", "Prepare for investor meeting").
The more parameters you provide, the more precisely the AI can target its synthesis.

Is the analysis process and my data kept confidential?

Absolute confidentiality is architected into the system. 1) Your specific query parameters and identifying information are processed in isolated, ephemeral environments. 2) The AI models used for synthesis (like Gemini 2.5 Pro via OpenRouter) are accessed via secure APIs and do not retain your specific query data for their own training post-request. 3) The proprietary backend processes operate solely on publicly available or licensed data sources and never interface with your internal systems. 4) All generated reports and associated metadata are stored encrypted at rest within our secure database infrastructure. We adhere to strict data privacy protocols. NDAs are available for enterprise clients requiring further assurance.

What is the output format and structure?

Both tiers provide professionally structured PDF reports designed for clarity and actionability. Key sections typically include:

  • Executive Summary: High-level findings and key takeaways (more strategic in Premium).
  • Analysis Sections: Detailed breakdown based on your query (e.g., Market Size & Growth, Competitor Profiles, Trend Analysis).
  • Data Visualizations: Charts and graphs illustrating key data points and trends.
  • Key Insights & Synthesis: AI-generated interpretation of the data and its implications.
  • Strategic Recommendations (Premium Only): Actionable options based on the analysis.
  • Source Appendix: References and links to key data sources used.
The Premium tier adds an Executive Briefing Deck (PPTX) and optional raw data exports (CSV/JSON).

Nexus Plan is optimized for high-speed, autonomous synthesis based on your initial objective definition. Direct interaction or iterative revisions are not part of the standard protocols to maintain velocity.
